Practice overview

I work at the intersection of law, technology, and public service.

My practice focuses on improving access to legal information and support, particularly for individuals who may face language, cultural, or system barriers. Through volunteering with organisations such as Citizens Advice and Age UK, I have supported clients with benefits, housing, and related legal processes, developing a careful, structured, and empathetic approach to problem-solving.

Alongside my legal training, I have a strong interest in how digital tools, automation, and clear communication can make legal services more accessible. I actively experiment with content creation, process design, and simple legal-tech ideas to reduce friction in complex procedures and help people better understand their rights.

I am especially interested in community-facing legal practice, policy-informed work, and roles that combine legal reasoning with communication, outreach, and innovation.
